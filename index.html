Mahatma Gandhi: A Life of Truth and Non-Violence

Welcome to the Tribute Page

This tribute page is dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi, the leader of the Indian independence movement, known for his philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience.

Explore his life, work, and lasting impact on the world.

Biography

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, commonly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India. He was a lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule.

Gandhi spent over 20 years in South Africa, where he developed his concept of Satyagraha (truth-force), a method of peaceful resistance that he would later employ to challenge British rule in India.

Key Achievements

  • Leader of the Indian National Congress.
  • Key figure in the Salt March of 1930, protesting against British salt taxes.
  • Instrumental in the Quit India Movement in 1942.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1869: Born in Porbandar, Gujarat, India.
  • 1888: Sailed to London to study law.
  • 1893: Arrived in South Africa to practice law and faced racial discrimination.
  • 1906: First implemented Satyagraha in South Africa.
  • 1915: Returned to India and joined the Indian National Congress.
  • 1930: Led the Salt March.
  • 1942: Launched the Quit India Movement.
  • 1947: India achieved independence.
  • 1948: Assassinated in New Delhi.

Philosophy of Non-Violence

Gandhi's philosophy was centered around Ahimsa (non-violence) and Satyagraha (truth and nonviolent resistance). He believed that oppression should be met with peaceful protest and civil disobedience rather than violence.

Famous Quotes

"You must be the change you wish to see in the world." - Mahatma Gandhi
"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others." - Mahatma Gandhi

Global Legacy

Gandhi’s legacy transcends India, inspiring numerous global movements for civil rights and freedom.
